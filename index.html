Mission Statement

If you and this dye were playing hide and seek, you would win every time, because this dye is everywhere! It is so common that a 2023 report from Amen Clinics stated that 6,000,000 pounds of red dye is used in products so far this year.

Like many other children in schools across the country, I have been adversely affected by Red Dye 40. This common food additive is widely used in food served in our schools, including lunches, which is causing distressing health issues in students, including myself.

Red Dye 40, an artificial food dye, has been linked to hyperactivity in children as well as immune system tumors, according to the Center for Science in the Public Interest (CSPI). Despite these alarming facts, it is still regularly used in foods that our children consume daily at school. It's high time we question if the color and appeal of food are more important than the wellbeing of our kids.

In Europe, products that contain Red Dye 40 are required to carry warnings stating that consumption could lead to hyperactivity in children (Per the Food Regulations 2010). Yet in the United States, we still allow our schools to use this harmful additive without any warning. This is both an inadequate and unjust practice, especially when children's health is at stake.

We demand that schools nationwide cease the use of Red Dye 40 in their lunches. Kids deserve healthier and safer meal options. Let's prioritize their well-being over colorful food. What is Red Dye 40?

Red Dye 40, also known as Allura Red AC, is a synthetic food dye commonly used in processed foods, beverages, candies, and medications. It has been linked to various health concerns, including hyperactivity in children, allergic reactions, and potential carcinogenic effects.
